**Slide 1: Mobile-First Social Commerce & The Payment Shift (Time: 45s)**

Now, let's examine the digital enablers behind Shopee's platform development. The first factor is mobile-first commerce. Shopee was designed primarily for mobile users rather than desktop, optimizing the interface for mobile navigation and short interaction cycles. This design supports its development into social commerce.

Instead of using the platform only for intentional shopping, users increasingly engage in browsing and content consumption. Features such as Shopee Live and Shopee Video integrate entertainment and shopping behavior, where user-generated content and KOC reviews contribute to purchase decisions and can shorten the conversion process.

In addition, one drawback in Southeast Asian markets is the continued reliance on cash-on-delivery due to trust and payment preferences. To address this, Shopee promotes digital payment adoption through ShopeePay and SPayLater, supported by incentives such as vouchers and delivery discounts, encouraging a gradual shift from cash-based to digital payments.

---

**Slide 2: Last-Mile Infrastructure & The AI Horizon (Time: 45s)**

The next enabler is last-mile delivery capability. In urban markets with high density and logistical complexity, delivery speed and reliability become operational challenges. Shopee addresses this by developing its own logistics arm, SPX Express, alongside third-party partners, which improves control over delivery performance and fulfillment efficiency.

Finally, data and artificial intelligence play a central role in system coordination. Shopee uses recommendation systems to personalize product exposure based on user behavior, such as search history and browsing patterns. In addition, visual search tools allow users to upload images to identify similar or matching products within the platform.

Overall, these mechanisms illustrate how infrastructure, payment systems, logistics, and AI work together to support platform functionality.
