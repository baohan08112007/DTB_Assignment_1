# Types of E-Commerce Transactions

| Transaction Type | Definition | Characteristics | Real-World Example |
|---|---|---|---|
| **B2C** *(Business-to-Consumer)* | Direct commercial transactions between businesses and individual consumers. | Small transaction volumes per order but features a very high frequency. | Buying a pair of shoes from the official Nike store on Shopee Mall. |
| **B2B** *(Business-to-Business)* | Commercial transactions conducted between businesses. | Typically involves large contract values, complex decision-making processes, and high-security platform requirements (such as an Extranet). | Nike purchasing tons of rubber from a raw material supplier, or an enterprise buying CRM management software. |
| **C2C** *(Consumer-to-Consumer)* | Direct transactions between consumers, empowered by the boom of digital platforms. | The platform purely acts as an intermediary providing the necessary infrastructure or payment gateways. | Trading secondhand goods on Cho Tot, participating in liquidation groups on Facebook, or transacting on eBay. |

---

## B2C — Business-to-Consumer

**Definition:** Direct commercial transactions between businesses and individual consumers.

**Characteristics:**
- Small transaction volumes per order
- Very high transaction frequency

**Real-World Example:** Buying a pair of shoes from the official Nike store on Shopee Mall.

---

## B2B — Business-to-Business

**Definition:** Commercial transactions conducted between businesses.

**Characteristics:**
- Typically involves large contract values
- Complex decision-making processes
- High-security platform requirements (such as an Extranet)

**Real-World Example:** Nike purchasing tons of rubber from a raw material supplier, or an enterprise buying CRM management software.

---

## C2C — Consumer-to-Consumer

**Definition:** Direct transactions between consumers, empowered by the boom of digital platforms.

**Characteristics:**
- The platform purely acts as an intermediary
- Provides the necessary infrastructure or payment gateways

**Real-World Example:** Trading secondhand goods on Cho Tot, participating in liquidation groups on Facebook, or transacting on eBay.
